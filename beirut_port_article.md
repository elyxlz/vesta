# This year, the state stood with the Aug. 4 victims

**PORT BLAST COMMEMORATION**

*L'Orient-Le Jour*  
By Suzanne BAAKLINI, Lilia GEHA, Nemtala EDDE  
August 5, 2025, 07:34

## Key Quote
Justice Minister Adel Nassar told L'Orient-Le Jour: **"I can't give a date, but from what I understand, the indictment is in the final stages."**

## The Fifth Anniversary: A Turning Point

The fifth commemoration of the Beirut port explosion brought together not only the families of the almost 250 victims and hundreds of citizens, but also a majority of ministers from Prime Minister Nawaf Salam's government. This strong show of support from state officials was notable, as Judge Tarek Bitar's investigation resumes after years of political obstruction and as recent political shifts have revived hopes in the fight against corruption and the pursuit of justice.

Even Health Minister Rakan Nasreddine, appointed by Hezbollah, was in attendance — a symbolic move, given the accusations leveled at the Hezbollah-Amal alliance for blocking the investigation.

From the podium facing the port, relatives of the victims acknowledged the political presence while expressing continued caution about the government's commitment to justice.

## Context: The 2025 Commemoration

### A Historic Shift in State Response

For the first time in five years since the catastrophic explosion that killed over 220 people and injured thousands, Lebanon's government demonstrated unprecedented support for the victims' families. This marks a dramatic departure from previous years when state institutions actively obstructed the investigation.

### Key Developments in 2025:

1. **National Day of Mourning**: Prime Minister Nawaf Salam officially declared August 4 as a day of national mourning
2. **Presidential Support**: President Joseph Aoun publicly committed to pursuing justice for the victims
3. **Judicial Progress**: Judge Tarek Bitar resumed his investigation after two years of forced suspension
4. **Monument Designation**: Culture Minister Ghassan Salameh declared the port silos as historic monuments, preserving them as a permanent memorial

### The Commemoration Events

The commemoration began with hundreds gathering in Karantina, near the Church of Notre-Dame de la Délivrance. Families of victims held a silent march from the church to the destroyed port, where they observed a minute of silence before the skeletal remains of the grain silos.

In a poignant gesture, 75 trees bearing the names of victims were planted in a memorial garden, creating a living monument to those lost in the explosion.

### Political Implications

The presence of government ministers, including those from parties previously accused of obstructing justice, signals a potential shift in Lebanon's political landscape. The new leadership under President Aoun and Prime Minister Salam appears committed to breaking the cycle of impunity that has characterized Lebanese politics.

Former security chiefs and high-ranking officials, who had previously refused to appear for questioning, are now showing up for interrogations - a development that would have been unthinkable in previous years.

### Ongoing Investigation

Judge Tarek Bitar's investigation, which had been suspended for over two years due to political interference, has resumed with renewed vigor. According to Justice Minister Adel Nassar, the indictment is in its final stages, though no specific timeline has been provided.

The investigation continues to face challenges, including:
- Political pressure from various factions
- International complications regarding the origin of the ammonium nitrate
- Questions about the involvement of senior political and security officials

### Families' Response

While acknowledging the government's newfound support, families of victims remain cautiously optimistic. Years of disappointment have taught them to be wary of political promises. They continue to demand:
- Full transparency in the investigation
- Accountability for all responsible parties, regardless of political affiliation
- International support to ensure an impartial investigation
- Preservation of evidence and the blast site

### International Context

The international community continues to monitor the situation closely. The European Union and United Nations have repeatedly called for a transparent and impartial investigation. France, which has a historical connection to Lebanon, has been particularly vocal in supporting the victims' families.

### Looking Forward

As Lebanon marks five years since its most devastating peacetime disaster, the question remains whether this newfound political will can translate into genuine accountability. The coming months will be crucial in determining whether the promises made during this year's commemoration will result in the justice that families have been demanding since August 4, 2020.
