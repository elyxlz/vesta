**Grade Weight:** 40% of total module grade

## Task Overview

Take a look at a question of significance to your client (Aspex Portsmouth) and apply a scenario or systems analysis around it over a period of time that you deem appropriate.

You are permitted to use diagrams as long as they relate clearly to your essay and analysis.

## Essay Structure

### Introduction (≤300 words)
- Introduce the domain you are looking at
- Demonstrate its relevance to the client (Aspex Portsmouth)

### Main Analysis (≈900 words)
- Introduce frameworks or methods of analysis you are using
- Explain why you are using that particular framework/method
- Present your analysis and predictions

### Conclusion (≤300 words)
- Your own take on what will happen
- Relate back to the client and what the client needs

## Key Advice

### 1. Choose a Sensible Domain
- **Not too narrow:** Should allow for broad prediction/analysis
- **Not too broad:** Should not require group work length
- **Examples for cloth manufacturer:** Production systems, geographic regions, international trade/tariffs, local demand trends
- **Must make clear link to client impact**

### 2. Choose Good Frameworks
- **PESTEL** is never a bad starting point
- Consider scenario and systems analysis methods from lectures
- For UK market clients: macro trends + electoral cycle interactions
- For technologies: challenges of getting technology to market

### 3. Method Over Answer
- Analysis will be speculative - being wrong is okay
- **Focus on reasoning process** not just outcomes
- Show awareness of where uncertainties lie
- Complex universe = unpredictable outcomes

### 4. Use Diagrams
- Show domain as system, hierarchy, or network
- Must relate clearly to essay and analysis

### 5. Use Data
- Include numbers when available
- Visualize data
- Make arguments based on data
- Use summaries (e.g., mean of predictions vs individual forecasts)

### 6. Interrogate Data
- Work out what data means
- What phenomena is it capturing?
- Does it capture it well or not?

### 7. Interpret Data Broadly
- Data doesn't just mean numbers
- Consider other forms of information

### 8. Structure Well
- Clear introduction as specified
- Body broken into paragraphs (one point per paragraph)
- Clear distinction between paragraphs
- First sentence makes the point, following sentences elaborate/present data

### 9. Don't Aim for Exhaustive Coverage
- A 'take' is sufficient
- Other opinions are available and acknowledged

### 10. Take an Opening Shot
- Start with ideas rather than trying to be comprehensive
- Get ideas down in some form
- Add critical commentary sparingly on most important issues only
