# BASC0023 - The Knowledge Economy: Module Overview

## Module Structure

The module combines **two types of learning**:

1. **Academic analysis** of the knowledge economy from two different disciplinary backgrounds:
   - Sociology/policy analysis
   - Management/economics

2. **Business consultancy** with student groups responding to requests from organisations for consultancy research projects

The two streams combine to develop skills in both **critical understanding of** and **ability to operate within** the knowledge economy.

## Assessment Structure

**Reflects the two streams:**
- **Group projects** (report and presentation) from consultancy work with external client
- **Individual essay** reflecting on the nature of the knowledge economy

## Consultancy Project Details

### Project Selection Process
- Teams of **4-5 students** on real-world consultancy projects
- Projects sourced by UCL through Jo Young's outreach to wide range of organisations
- **Selection process**: Students submit **top 6 project preferences** in order before start of term
- **Allocation**: Module convenors do their best to allocate highest possible choice, but **cannot guarantee first choice**
- **Consequence**: If preferences not submitted by due date, will be allocated to a group
- **Project scope**: More generally "application of interdisciplinary research skills to an organisation outside of academia" - could be business consultancy OR fundamental research for a campaign

### Group Structure
- Each **seminar = 3 project groups**
- Each group allocated a **'Project Manager'** from UCL module teaching team
- Project Manager serves as **seminar tutor**

### Project Timeline & Expectations
- **Week 1**: Contact client to introduce yourselves and set first meeting
- **Ongoing**: Meet client **once a week** throughout module
- **Continuous**: Progress consultancy research all through module
- **Target**: Complete research and begin writing up report by **beginning of December**

## Module Phases

### Phase 1: Till Reading Week (Beginning of November) + One Session After
- **2 lectures per week** (one from Peter, one from Pete) about the knowledge economy
- **1 hour seminar** per week
- **Initiate and develop** group consultancy project

### Phase 2: Post-Reading Week
- **Teaching focuses solely** on group consultancy project
- **Seminars continue** for whole term
- **Lectures finish** by week after reading week

## Schedule Overview

- **From Week 1**: 2 lectures/week + 1 hour seminar
- **Lectures end**: Week after reading week (early November)
- **Seminars**: Continue for whole term
- **Project work**: Continuous throughout module
