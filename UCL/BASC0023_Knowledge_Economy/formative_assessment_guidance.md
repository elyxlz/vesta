# TKE: Workplan Guidance 2025-26

## Formative Assessment Overview

**Important**: This is formative assessment - we will give feedback on the task and the project but will not be giving an overall mark, and this report will not count towards your grade. Completing this task will ensure that you receive clear guidance from client and project manager and can complete the report in a timely manner. Though formative you should consider this an important component of the module.

If there are any concerns that completing the workplan may negatively affect progression of your consultancy project, ensure you discuss this as early as possible with your project manager and seminar tutor.

You may agree a submission deadline with your project manager. The important thing to bear in mind is that first, this exercise is for your practical benefit, and second that doing it to time is optimal.

## What Would We Like You To Do?

The purpose of this task is to get you to spell out what you agreed to do with your client, and to communicate it to us as if we were overall senior management and therefore had limited bandwidth to take in all the nuances of every project at this stage. (Note: we're not senior management really, and we will take in all the project details, but in learning how to navigate workplace setting it's worth getting used to communicating succinctly with your managers especially as you will usually have more information about the project than they do. And note that all kinds of organisations including Government, NGO, charity and campaigning have managers, though not always called that.)

You should meet with your client by week 1 to discuss the initial brief you have been given and inform the client of your expertise. Over the next 2-3 weeks you should organise your group and in discussion with client and project manager you should define the project. You may wish to discuss with your seminar tutor an early and informal presentation of your project and the issues you see in defining it to your seminar; if you wish to do so discuss with your seminar tutor. Your task in this early phase is to quickly test information sources and define the project more exactly than the initial brief does.

## What We Would Like To See…

We would like your report to be **no more than three pages** including any chart or task breakdown.

## Recommended Workplan Considerations:

1. **Who is the client and how does the project fit into their organisation and any strategy they have discussed with you?**

2. **What is the nature of the project?**

3. **What are the key information sources for the project? What testing of information availability has been done?**

4. **What are the key things that you would like to achieve with this project?**

5. **What difficulties might you anticipate?**

6. **What solutions do you think you might find to any challenges that you have identified in your initial meetings?**

7. **What are the major milestones for the project.**

Beyond this, anything that you think we might need to know would be helpful, but it's more important that both you and we have a clearly communicated outline of the project going forward.

## Feedback Process

We will aim to give feedback to you as quickly as possible. Generally, we'll do it in the next seminar.
